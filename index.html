Case Study

ADNOC Distribution — Dynamic Customer Journey Orchestration

Salesforce Marketing Cloud · Personalization Studio · Comarch Loyalty · Journey & Automation Studio

Challenge

Design a reusable orchestration platform capable of dynamically assigning customers to the next best journey and offer across multiple business lines, while respecting consent, reducing communication costs, maintaining complete auditability, and supporting commercial decision making.

Stakeholders

Business directors, analytics, F&B, FMCG, station operations, loyalty operations (Comarch offer setup) and technology — with me as the single point of accountability between the business and delivery, securing approvals through face-to-face engagement.

Architecture thinking

Treat customer engagement as a continuous decisioning system rather than a collection of independent campaigns.

Instead of building isolated Journey Builder flows, design a reusable decision framework capable of determining the customer's next best journey, next best offer, communication eligibility, suppression state, and personalization across multiple channels.

Technical solution

Designed and implemented a reusable journey orchestration platform that dynamically determines the customer's next best journey, offer eligibility, communication channels, consent handling, suppression state, and mobile app banner visibility. Built reusable logging, historical tracking, and campaign intelligence layers that provide complete traceability for customer participation, voucher redemptions, and operational auditing across Salesforce Marketing Cloud and Comarch Loyalty.

Execution

Delivered the platform across ADNOC Distribution's C-Store retail business. Led solution architecture, implementation, stakeholder engagement, production delivery, campaign analytics, and ongoing optimisation from commercial brief through post-campaign reporting.

Lessons learned
Design for the second campaign, not the first.

Instead of building campaign-specific logic, invest in reusable constructs such as aggregate attributes, suppression frameworks, logging models, and orchestration rules that compound in value over time.

Automation timing is part of system architecture.

Cross-platform automations require deliberate execution windows. Allow sufficient processing time between systems and avoid midnight execution where transaction cut-offs, timezone differences and upstream processing can introduce inconsistent data.

Auditability should be designed, not added later.

Logging, customer traceability and operational reporting should be planned during solution design—not after go-live. Customer support, campaign operations, analytics and compliance all depend on trustworthy historical data.

Outcome

    5.2M+ customer engagements across a five-month campaign portfolio.
    270K+ customer conversions delivered through behaviour-driven journey orchestration.
    Up to 3× higher response rates versus campaign targets.
    2×+ forecast incremental gross profit achieved on flagship campaigns.
    AED 15K–20K monthly communication cost savings through intelligent channel optimisation.

Case Study

Al Jasser / Arabian Oud — Multi-Country Customer Service Orchestration

Salesforce Service Cloud · Unifonic WhatsApp · Web-to-Case · Email-to-Case · Apex · LWC · Omni-Channel

Challenge

Scale customer service from a single Saudi Salesforce org into GCC and international markets, unifying WhatsApp, web, and email case entry under one governed, country-aware routing framework across multiple brands.

Stakeholders

Stakeholders included Al Jasser customer care, CRM performance, IT, and digital leadership teams, together with Digitas programme, business, and Salesforce delivery teams. I operated as the hands-on Salesforce technical lead connecting business priorities, integration constraints, Salesforce implementation, testing, deployment, documentation, and handover.

Architecture thinking

Treat customer service as a reusable orchestration layer: every inbound channel should create a Salesforce case, classify it by country and channel, route it through a controlled assignment framework, and preserve agent visibility through Omni-Channel.

Technical solution

Designed and implemented a reusable Salesforce Service Cloud orchestration layer that connected WhatsApp, Web-to-Case, and Email-to-Case into a single country-aware case management model. The solution used shared country fields, queue structures, dummy-queue staging, assignment rules, Omni-Channel handling, Apex automation, Unifonic WhatsApp integration, survey sending, and account-level prompt-template functionality to support both GCC and international customer service operations.

Execution

Delivered the rollout through a structured managed-services model, covering requirements clarification, stakeholder coordination, Salesforce configuration, Apex and flow updates, Web-to-Case and Email-to-Case setup, WhatsApp/Unifonic alignment, UAT support, production deployment, documentation, and handover. Acted as the hands-on Salesforce technical lead connecting client priorities, integration dependencies, delivery governance, and operational readiness across multiple country launches.

Lessons learned

Multi-country service rollouts succeed when routing is treated as a reusable operating model, not as separate channel logic. Staging queues, shared fields, assignment rules, and full-path UAT help protect agent capacity, improve operational control, and make expansion into new countries easier — but client-side dependencies, data hygiene, and divergent business flows must be managed early as part of the architecture, not left as post-launch cleanup.

Outcome

7 GCC markets launched with Salesforce Service Cloud support across WhatsApp, Web-to-Case and Email-to-Case.
6 international markets added to the support model, extending the same country-aware routing pattern beyond the original GCC rollout.
3 service channels unified into a single Salesforce case-orchestration framework with queue routing, country capture, Omni-Channel handling and Unifonic integration.
USD 76K managed-services engagement supported through a 6-month delivery model with 105.6 Salesforce development hours/month.
5M+ record data-hygiene issue identified and supported through remediation planning, separating valid operational records from historical testing-phase data.

Case Study

Dubai Tourism — Marketing Technology Architecture

SFMC · Custom API Integration · Journey Builder · CloudPages · Dynamic Content

Challenge

A high-visibility national tourism brand needed external website and campaign data to flow reliably into Salesforce Marketing Cloud, trigger customer journeys, support campaign reporting, and reduce recurring manual effort across campaign launches and handover operations.

Stakeholders

Dubai Tourism CRM, digital platform, campaign, reporting and website stakeholders, together with Digitas delivery and Marketing Cloud teams responsible for implementation, troubleshooting, reporting, handover and sign-off support.

Architecture thinking

Remove the manual middle. Let external website interactions flow directly into Marketing Cloud through governed API and CloudPage patterns, then use Journey Builder and automation to process, trigger, report and hand over campaign operations consistently.

Technical solution

Built API-triggered Marketing Cloud journeys and CloudPage-based processing patterns to receive website payloads, process JSON parameters, store submissions in data extensions, and trigger personalized customer journeys. Supported dynamic overlays, traveller journeys, campaign reporting, data cleanup, triggered emails, and reusable handover documentation across multiple Dubai Tourism campaign workstreams.

Execution

Delivered hands-on SFMC development across API setup, Journey Builder configuration, CloudPages, data extensions, reporting automation, campaign troubleshooting, stakeholder coordination, technical documentation, walkthroughs and acceptance support from implementation through handover.

Lessons learned

Marketing operations scale better when campaign intake, API ingestion, reporting and handover are designed as reusable operating patterns rather than one-off fixes. The architecture must make external data reliable, visible and testable before campaigns reach production.

Outcome

6 API-triggered traveller journeys activated for testing across submission input, POI, itinerary, deals and offers, events recommendation and share-with-friends use cases.
4-step Summer 2024 triggered journey structured across immediate, 5-day, 7-day and 14-day follow-up emails.
CloudPage API workaround implemented to keep website form data flowing into Marketing Cloud despite CORS limitations.
3 DEF 2024 data extensions supported through stress-test cleanup, backup, cross-checking and automation reactivation activity.
Recurring reporting automation supported for weekly submissions, country-based reporting and language-based reporting.

Thinking & Playbooks

How I work.

Campaigns are systems, not sends.

A campaign should not depend on hero effort every launch. I design reusable campaign constructs — segmentation, suppression, offer logic, tracking and reporting — so the next launch is faster, safer and easier to govern than the first.

Bad data becomes bad decisions.

Data quality is not a back-office issue. If customer status, consent, eligibility or exclusions are wrong, every downstream campaign decision becomes unreliable. I treat data defects as business-risk defects, not just technical cleanup.

The architect should stay close to the build.

Architecture only works if it survives real implementation. I stay hands-on with AMPscript, SQL, SSJS, Apex, APIs, CloudPages and platform constraints so solution decisions are grounded in what can actually be built, tested and supported.

Stakeholder alignment is part of the architecture.

The best technical design still fails without approvals, operational readiness and clear ownership. I treat business sign-off, delivery dependencies, testing, documentation and handover as part of the system — not admin work after the build.
