Case Study

ADNOC Distribution — Enterprise Loyalty Architecture

Salesforce Marketing Cloud · Personalization Studio · Data Cloud · Comarch Loyalty · Journey & Automation Studio

Challenge

A national, multi-business loyalty programme — fuel, F&B, FMCG, convenience and station operations — running on a Comarch + Salesforce stack with legacy data defects, fragile lifecycle journeys, and campaign setup processes that didn't scale.

Stakeholders

Business directors, analytics, F&B, FMCG, station operations, loyalty operations (Comarch offer setup) and technology — with me as the single point of accountability between the business and delivery, securing approvals through face-to-face engagement.

Architecture thinking

Treat campaigns as systems, not sends. Trace every defect to its root in the integration layer rather than patching symptoms. Replace one-off builds with reusable constructs that compound: segmentation pages, exclusion frameworks, and behaviour-driven journey orchestration that positions each customer on their next best journey based on behaviour and consent.

Technical solution

The SFMC tech stack — Journey Builder, Automation Studio, Personalization Studio — wired to Comarch through the integration layer; SQL and AMPscript segmentation; a reusable emirate-based Cloud Page; and an always-on journey tracker with rotating access keys and logging that removed manual log handoffs to call-centre agents.

Execution

End-to-end ownership from commercial brief through launch and reporting. Diagnosed and fixed a year-old Comarch↔Salesforce sync defect that only updated active records, corrupting customer status in the master campaign database. Repaired a birthday-journey defect affecting ~4,000 customers with a same-day fix. Rebuilt mobile scrub and exclusion logic end-to-end (~3,000 invalid records corrected) with full documentation and flow diagrams.

Lessons learned

A defect that survives a year isn't a code problem — it's a monitoring and ownership problem. And reusable constructs aren't just efficiency; they're how you buy back the time to find the defects in the first place.

Outcome

+112% daily registrations, +500 daily transactions and AED 30,000 incremental gross profit from a single flagship campaign. 60–90 hours of operational effort recovered monthly. Restored data integrity and stakeholder trust across the loyalty estate.

Case Study

Dubai Tourism — Marketing Technology Architecture

SFMC · Custom API Integration · Journey Builder · Dynamic Content

Challenge

A high-visibility national tourism brand needed lead generation and engagement journeys connected to external systems — without the manual effort that was holding the marketing operation back.

Stakeholders

The national tourism marketing team and the external lead-source systems feeding their pipeline.

Architecture thinking

Remove the manual middle. Let external lead data flow straight into journeys, and let email templates assemble themselves from source data rather than from a person's time.

Technical solution

Custom API integrations feeding lead data into SFMC; dynamic email template systems driven by source data; and Automation Studio rework to remove recurring manual steps.

Execution

Designed and built the integrations and template systems for personalised engagement at scale, then streamlined the surrounding automation so the team stopped re-doing the same work each campaign.

Lessons learned

Personalisation at scale is a templating problem before it's a content problem. Solve the assembly and the content takes care of itself.

Outcome

Higher engagement on dynamic content and a measurable reduction in manual campaign effort. [METRIC NEEDED — add engagement % or hours-saved figures when available]

Case Study

Inchcape PLC — APAC Marketing Cloud Transformation

Multi-market SFMC · SQL · AMPscript · SSJS · Third-party Booking Integration

Challenge

A global automotive distributor needed consistent, locally-relevant Marketing Cloud solutions across APAC markets — each with its own data realities, languages and third-party systems.

Stakeholders

APAC market teams, third-party booking platforms, and regional data owners — each with different constraints.

Architecture thinking

One pattern, many markets. Build market-aware solutions that survive local data realities and languages rather than bespoke builds per country.

Technical solution

SQL, AMPscript and SSJS at depth; a two-way booking-system integration with third-party sites; and managed multi-market SFMC deployments.

Execution

Led the APAC solution build over two years, owning the depth work and keeping deployments consistent as the footprint grew across markets.

Lessons learned

Multi-market is a deployment-risk problem. The win is in repeatability, not cleverness.

Thinking & Playbooks

How I work.

Campaigns are systems, not sends.

A campaign that works once is luck. A campaign construct that works every time is architecture. I design for the hundredth launch, not the first.

Data defects are trust defects.

When a sync bug silently mismarks customers for a year, the cost isn't technical — it's every decision made on bad data. Root-cause discipline is a commercial skill.

The architect should be able to build it.

Diagrams that can't survive contact with AMPscript, Apex, or an API rate limit aren't architecture. Staying hands-on keeps designs honest.

Stakeholders are part of the system.

Approvals, operational readiness and face-to-face accountability are architectural concerns. The best design fails if the business never signs it off.
